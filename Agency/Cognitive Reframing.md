## Brief Outline

### Introduction
- "I can't afford it" / "I can't do that" / "I can't change"
- The word "can't" is a cage you built yourself
- Most limitations aren't real - they're beliefs masquerading as facts
- The reframe that changes everything: "I can't" → "What if I could?"

### Part 1: The Anatomy of Limiting Beliefs
- **Disguised as facts**: "I'm not a morning person" feels true but isn't
- **Self-fulfilling**: Believing you can't means you won't try, which proves you "can't"
- **Socially reinforced**: Others confirm your limitations to feel better about their own
- **Comfortable**: Limitations excuse inaction and protect from failure
- Common limiting beliefs:
  - "I'm too old/young"
  - "I don't have time/money/connections"
  - "I'm not the type of person who..."
  - "People like me don't..."
- The test: Is this actually impossible, or just hard/scary/unfamiliar?

### Part 2: The Neuroscience of Belief
- Your brain treats beliefs as reality and filters evidence accordingly
- Confirmation bias: You see what you expect to see
- Reticular activating system: Your brain highlights what you focus on
- When you believe "I can't," your brain stops looking for solutions
- When you believe "Maybe I can," your brain starts problem-solving
- Research: Growth mindset vs. fixed mindset outcomes
- The physical reality: beliefs change neural pathways

### Part 3: The Reframing Formula
- **Step 1: Catch the limiting belief**
  - Notice when you say/think "I can't"
  - Write it down
- **Step 2: Challenge it**
  - Is this actually impossible or just difficult?
  - Have others done this? If yes, it's not impossible
  - What evidence supports this belief? What contradicts it?
- **Step 3: Reframe it**
  - "I can't" → "I haven't learned how yet"
  - "I can't" → "What if I could?"
  - "I can't" → "What would I need to do differently?"
  - "I can't afford it" → "How could I afford it?"
- **Step 4: Take one small action** aligned with the reframe
  - Reframing without action is just wishful thinking
  - Action cements the new belief

### Part 4: Strategic Self-Delusion
- The fine line between delusional and visionary
- Successful people often start with "unrealistic" beliefs
- Elon Musk, SpaceX, and useful delusion
- The question isn't "Is this belief true?" but "Is this belief useful?"
- Sometimes you need to believe before you have evidence
- Fake it till you make it - the research backs it up
- When to embrace strategic delusion vs. when to be realistic

### Part 5: Reframing Common Agency Blockers
- **"I don't have time"** → "How could I make time?" / "What's actually the priority?"
- **"I don't know how"** → "How could I learn?"
- **"I'm not qualified"** → "What would make me qualified, and how do I get it?"
- **"It's too late"** → "Too late compared to what? Who decides?"
- **"People will judge me"** → "And what if they do? How does that actually affect my life?"
- **"I might fail"** → "What if I succeed? What if failure teaches me?"
- **"I'm not that type of person"** → "What if I could become that type of person?"

### Part 6: Practical Reframing Exercises
- **The "What if I could?" journal**: Daily practice reframing one limitation
- **The evidence hunt**: Actively look for examples of your reframe being true
- **The 10x question**: "What would I do if I had 10x the agency I do now?"
- **The alternate timeline**: "What would someone who believes they can do this, do next?"
- **The identity shift**: "What would [someone you admire] do in this situation?"

### Part 7: Avoiding Toxic Positivity
- Reframing isn't denying reality
- Some things genuinely are impossible (you can't fly by flapping your arms)
- The difference:
  - **Toxic positivity**: "Just think positive and everything will work out!"
  - **Cognitive reframing**: "This is hard, and I'm capable of figuring it out"
- Acknowledge the obstacle while maintaining agency
- "This sucks AND I can handle it" beats "This doesn't suck!" or "I can't handle this"

### Part 8: The Compound Effect of Reframing
- Each reframe makes the next one easier
- Your default narrative shifts from "I can't" to "How could I?"
- You start seeing possibilities where you used to see walls
- The opportunities you notice multiply
- Over time, you become someone who solves problems instead of accepting limitations

### Conclusion
- Your beliefs are the biggest barrier to your agency
- "I can't" is almost always "I haven't yet" or "I'm scared to"
- The reframe that changes everything: "What if I could?"
- Start small: reframe one limiting belief this week
- Take one action aligned with the new belief
- Your brain will start believing what your actions demonstrate
- Agency isn't about being fearless; it's about reframing fear as a puzzle to solve
