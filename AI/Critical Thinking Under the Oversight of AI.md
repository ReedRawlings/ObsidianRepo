1. Key Findings from the Research (300-400 words)
The studies main goal was to understand when and how knowledge workers leverage critical thinking when using AI and why that effort changes. 

They found that workers engage critically when confident in themselves and their understanding. Less so when they trusted the output of AI. 

- Overview: Summarize the study’s main goal—understanding when/how workers enact critical thinking with GenAI (RQ1) and why effort changes (RQ2), based on a survey of 319 knowledge workers.
    
- RQ1: When and How Critical Thinking Happens:
    
    - Workers engage critically when confident in themselves, not AI—less when they over-trust GenAI (Page 7).
        
    - Actions: Setting goals, verifying outputs, assessing quality (Section 4.1, Page 7).
        
    - Barriers: Time pressure, low awareness, or “not my job” vibes (Table 5, Page 23).
        
- RQ2: Why Effort Changes with GenAI:
    
    - GenAI reduces effort (e.g., info gathering) but shifts it to verification/stewardship (Page 2, Section 6.2).
        
    - Risk: Overreliance could erode independent thinking long-term (Section 7, Page 16).
        
    - Confidence drives this—high AI trust = less effort, high self-confidence = more (Section 6.1.1, Page 14).
        
- Dunning-Kruger Connection: Add a tidbit—overconfident novices might over-rely on AI, while skilled workers dig deeper, echoing the effect’s miscalibration (Section 6.3, Page 15).
